## TLDR

Explainable AI should be treated as an interactive, educational process rather than a static product delivered to the user.

## Body

The current paradigm of Explainable AI (XAI) often treats explanations as static products or outputs to be delivered to the user. According to the text, this approach is reaching its limits because it fails to account for how humans actually process, internalize, and act upon new information.

To overcome this, researchers propose a paradigm shift that integrates established educational and learning theories into the XAI lifecycle. By treating explanations as "learning artifacts," the focus shifts from merely providing an algorithmic answer to facilitating a comprehensive learning process for the human user.

This learning-centric lifecycle is broken down into three distinct stages mirroring real-world knowledge acquisition: **Seeking** (driven by user motivation and goals), **Receiving** (focused on cognitive engagement), and **Producing** (encouraging users to generate their own understanding).

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Treating XAI as a learning process may significantly increase the cognitive load and time required for users to interact with AI systems. In high-stakes, time-sensitive environments (e.g., emergency medical triage or real-time financial trading), users may not have the luxury of engaging in a multi-stage learning process and may genuinely need a rapid, static "product" explanation.
