## TLDR

The cognitive and administrative burden required by a human researcher to guide an AI agent through the process of producing high-quality research.

## Body

The 'Human Steering Tax' refers to the extensive effort required by researchers to guide current agentic systems from an initial idea to a completed manuscript. This tax encompasses the oversight needed for tasks like literature reviews, proof sketches, experimental code generation, and continuous redrafting.

In existing systems like 'The AI Scientist,' this tax manifests as thousands of tokens of interaction and constant prompting to ensure the output remains mathematically sound and rigorous. This high steering tax acts as a bottleneck, limiting the true autonomy and efficiency of AI in the research lifecycle by forcing the human into a tedious micromanagement role.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

The concept assumes that human steering is purely a 'tax' or administrative burden, whereas iterative prompting and oversight are often the exact mechanisms through which actual scientific synthesis, course correction, and true novelty are injected by the human researcher. Removing this steering entirely might degrade the fundamental quality and originality of the scientific output.
