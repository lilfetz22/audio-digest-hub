## TLDR

Weaker, cheaper AI models can achieve near-parity with stronger models when seeded with the stronger model's accumulated institutional knowledge.

## Body

Knowledge transfer allows the insights and discoveries generated by a highly capable (and often expensive) model to be utilized by a less capable, more cost-effective model. By seeding the weaker model with the "institutional knowledge" of the stronger one, the performance gap between the two can be drastically reduced.

In practical applications, this approach has been shown to close coverage gaps significantly (e.g., from 6.6% down to 1.1%), halve operational costs, and reduce the time to convergence. Furthermore, it helps calibrate the judgment of independent agents, ensuring multiple runs converge on the exact same estimates and outcomes.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

This approach requires an initial upfront cost of running the expensive model to generate the seed knowledge. Furthermore, the weaker model may lack the reasoning capacity to properly utilize complex institutional knowledge, potentially misinterpreting the seeded data in edge cases.
