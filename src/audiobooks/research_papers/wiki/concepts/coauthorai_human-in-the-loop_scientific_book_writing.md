## TLDR

A collaborative AI system designed to assist domain experts in writing scientific books by keeping the human in control while offloading synthesis.

## Body

CoAuthorAI is a Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) system specifically engineered to address the limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs) in long-form academic writing. While LLMs excel at drafting short summaries, they struggle with book-length projects, often losing narrative coherence and hallucinating citations.

To solve this, CoAuthorAI operates as a collaborative workspace rather than an autonomous generator. It keeps the domain expert in the driver's seat to ensure rigorous accuracy, while the AI handles the heavy lifting of synthesizing information. The system achieves this through a modular architecture consisting of hierarchical outlining, content compression, and vector-based citation linking.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Human-in-the-loop systems still require significant manual effort and domain expertise. The system's effectiveness relies heavily on the expert's ability to guide the AI, structure the initial outline, and verify the final outputs, meaning it does not entirely remove the bottleneck of human labor in book writing.
