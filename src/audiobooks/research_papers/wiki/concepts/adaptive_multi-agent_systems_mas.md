## TLDR

Dynamic frameworks for multi-agent systems that autonomously adjust agent roles and communication topologies based on task requirements.

## Body

Adaptive MAS represents a shift from static, hard-coded agent pipelines to flexible architectures. In these systems, agents are empowered to determine their own communication structures and task assignments in real-time. The goal is to create more efficient, context-aware systems that can solve complex problems by dynamically reorganizing their internal workflows.

By moving away from pre-defined communication paths, these systems aim to optimize resource allocation and improve performance across diverse task domains. This adaptability is intended to make agents more capable of handling novel scenarios that fall outside of their initial design parameters.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

The primary criticism of adaptive MAS is that the optimization process often leads to brittle architectures. While these systems excel on training data, they frequently lack the stability required for production environments, as dynamic restructuring can introduce unpredictable failure modes.
