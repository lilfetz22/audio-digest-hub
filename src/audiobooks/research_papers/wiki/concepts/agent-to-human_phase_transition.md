## TLDR

A critical threshold exists where a software system's reliability abruptly collapses because human reviewers can no longer maintain an accurate mental model of the agent-driven ecosystem.

## Body

The Agent-to-Human Phase Transition describes a critical tipping point in human-AI collaborative systems. As the ratio of autonomous AI agents to human developers increases, the system approaches a "critical agent-to-human ratio," estimated by Russo to be somewhere between one and three agents per human.

Once this threshold is crossed, the system undergoes a phase transition. The sheer volume, speed, and complexity of agent-driven changes exceed the cognitive limits of human reviewers. Consequently, humans lose their mental model of the ecosystem, leading to a sudden and severe collapse in system reliability and oversight.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

The exact ratio (e.g., 1 to 3) is likely highly variable depending on the tooling, the autonomy level of the agents, and the specific domain. Better abstraction layers, automated testing, and AI-assisted summarization tools could push this cognitive threshold much higher, allowing humans to manage larger swaths of agents safely.
