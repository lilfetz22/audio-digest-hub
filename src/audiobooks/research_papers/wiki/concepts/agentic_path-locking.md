## TLDR

The tendency of AI agents in iterative loops to increasingly rely on their own previously generated entities, leading to conceptual calcification.

## Body

"Path-locking" is a critical risk in iterative AI workflows where agents progressively narrow their focus, leading to the calcification of an idea. As iterations progress, agents exhibit a strong tendency to rely on "inherited" entities—concepts, structures, or terminology—drawn directly from their own previous outputs rather than exploring new conceptual territory.

An analysis of entity recombination reveals that this feedback loop causes the generated ideas to become increasingly rigid. Because the agents continuously recycle the same foundational elements, the potential for true novelty diminishes, and the system becomes trapped in a self-reinforcing technical trajectory.

To mitigate this risk, system designers must actively intervene in the agentic loop. This can be achieved by explicitly injecting "entropy" into the system, forcing the agents to pull in fresh, disparate knowledge at later stages of the workflow. This disruption breaks the cycle of inheritance and maintains the conceptual flexibility needed for innovative idea generation.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Forcing entropy or injecting disparate knowledge in later stages risks derailing the coherence of the idea, potentially undoing the structural soundness achieved in earlier iterations. There is a delicate balance between preventing calcification and maintaining logical consistency that is difficult to automate reliably.
