## TLDR

In software systems heavily reliant on AI agents, architectural entropy grows at an accelerated rate compared to systems managed solely by human teams.

## Body

Entropy Acceleration is a proposition suggesting that the introduction of autonomous agents into a software development lifecycle fundamentally alters the rate of system degradation. While all software systems naturally accumulate technical debt and architectural entropy over time, agent-heavy systems experience this phenomenon at a much faster pace.

This acceleration is hypothesized to occur because agents generate code, make structural changes, and introduce dependencies at a volume and speed that outpaces traditional human development. Without rigorous architectural constraints, these micro-changes rapidly compound into significant structural disorder.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

This hypothesis assumes that agents lack the capacity for architectural foresight or self-refactoring. Future iterations of AI agents might be explicitly optimized to reduce architectural entropy, potentially reversing this trend. Additionally, empirical data comparing long-term human versus agent entropy rates is currently limited.
