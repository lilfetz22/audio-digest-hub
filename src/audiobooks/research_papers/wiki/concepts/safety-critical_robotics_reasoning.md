## TLDR

The integration of high-level semantic reasoning with rigid, verifiable mathematical frameworks for high-stakes environments like spacecraft operations.

## Body

Bridging the gap between human-like, intuitive reasoning and the deterministic, safety-critical constraints of aerospace operations is a foundational challenge in robotics. High-level agents must operate within bounds that ensure mission safety, requiring a hybrid approach that reconciles the flexibility of large language models with the rigidity of classical control theory.

This research emphasizes the need for 'production-grade' systems that can translate abstract goals into actionable, verifiable mathematical steps. This ensures that while an agent may have the capability for high-level reasoning, its final actions remain bound by mathematical safety guarantees essential for hazardous or high-stakes environments.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Integrating semantic reasoning into safety-critical loops introduces a massive verification burden. Proving that an AI agent will always output a mathematically safe command, regardless of the prompt input, remains an open research problem in formal methods.
