## TLDR

The rise of autonomous agents requires abandoning traditional artifact-level governance in favor of treating software as living, unstable ecosystems.

## Body

Core software engineering assumptions that have governed the industry for fifty years are being challenged by the advent of autonomous agents. Traditional "artifact-level governance"—which focuses on static outputs like individual files or isolated build artifacts—is no longer sufficient to maintain system integrity.

According to the research, software ecosystems must now be treated as living, breathing, and potentially unstable organisms. This paradigm shift requires a foundational change in how we think about code stability, relying heavily on ecosystem dynamics and information theory rather than just looking at isolated CI/CD pipelines.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Viewing software as an unstable biological organism might introduce unacceptable levels of risk for mission-critical or safety-critical systems. In these high-stakes environments, deterministic behavior and strict artifact-level verification are often legally or practically mandated, making a purely "ecosystem" approach unfeasible.
