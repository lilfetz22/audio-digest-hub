## TLDR

The systemic limitation in current LLM-based coding tools where interactions are forced into a single, sequential stream, hindering exploratory programming.

## Body

The Linearity Trap describes the friction caused by chat-based AI interfaces that enforce a sequential paradigm: prompt, generate, accept, and proceed. This structure mirrors a simple conversation but fails to capture the messy, iterative reality of professional programming, which often requires concurrent testing of different architectures or debugging strategies.

Because developers are constrained by this linear history, they are often forced to 'outsource' their comprehension. This leads to a scenario where users accept AI-generated code blocks without fully grasping how those snippets integrate into the larger codebase, ultimately reducing the developer's agency and architectural oversight.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Critics might argue that linear chat interfaces provide simplicity and reduced cognitive barriers for beginners or for tasks that are inherently simple. The 'trap' may be a deliberate design choice aimed at lowering the threshold for AI adoption rather than a technical oversight.
