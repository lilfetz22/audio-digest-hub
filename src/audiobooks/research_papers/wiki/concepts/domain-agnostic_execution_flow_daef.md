## TLDR

DAEF is a standardized structural framework that enforces consistency in task execution logic, enabling cross-domain skill transferability.

## Body

The Domain-Agnostic Execution Flow (DAEF) serves as a skeletal architecture for organizing task performance. By abstracting the logical steps required to complete a variety of operations into a universal format, it ensures that an agent does not need to relearn the fundamental procedural structure when shifting between different task domains.

This standardization allows for the aggregation of 166 disparate tasks into a unified learning environment. Because the underlying 'logic' remains invariant, the model can extract generalized patterns of action that are applicable regardless of the specific domain, facilitating higher efficiency in skill acquisition.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

The primary limitation of a domain-agnostic approach is the potential loss of task-specific nuances. By forcing all tasks into a rigid skeletal structure, the agent may struggle with highly specialized operations that require unique logic not captured by the DAEF template.
