## TLDR

A collaborative paradigm where human users interact with and guide the decision-making process of AI agents through visual, non-linear interfaces rather than passive monitoring.

## Body

Agentic steering shifts the role of the developer from a prompt-writer to an active curator of AI-driven outcomes. By utilizing graph-based representations, users can intervene at specific decision nodes in the agent's workflow, steering the system back toward desired objectives or correcting sub-optimal paths before the agent completes a long-running, multi-step task.

This approach emphasizes transparency and control. It moves away from the 'black box' nature of automated agents by forcing the agent's decision tree to be an explicit artifact, allowing for real-time adjustments and the recombination of different strategic paths.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Active steering requires constant human vigilance, which may negate the benefit of using an autonomous agent in the first place. There is a risk that 'manual intervention' could introduce human bias or errors into an otherwise optimized automated process, potentially hindering the agent's ability to explore solutions that are counter-intuitive but effective.
