## TLDR

The practice of formalizing research processes into defined, modular 'Skill Libraries' that govern agentic reasoning and validation steps.

## Body

Rather than providing agents with generic tools, skill-based orchestration involves defining end-to-end workflows that encapsulate best practices. By structuring research into a library of repeatable 'skills,' developers ensure that agents execute tasks consistently and follow predetermined validation and planning logic.

This paradigm shifts the burden from training better models to improving the structural orchestration of the research process. It transforms the agent from a general-purpose conversationalist into a reproducible, process-driven research assistant that follows an organization's specific operational standards.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Hard-coding workflows into 'Skill Libraries' may limit the agent's ability to improvise or handle novel edge cases that fall outside the predefined research process. Over-formalization can lead to rigid agents that struggle when the input domain or the nature of the research problem deviates from the standard library definition.
