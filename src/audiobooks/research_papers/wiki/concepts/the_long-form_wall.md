## TLDR

The tendency of Large Language Models to lose narrative consistency, hallucinate citations, and struggle with structure over book-length projects.

## Body

The "Long-Form Wall" refers to the architectural and functional limitations that Large Language Models face when generating extensive texts, such as books. While these models are highly capable of generating short-form content like emails or brief summaries, their performance degrades over long contexts.

When pushed beyond their optimal generation lengths, LLMs tend to lose the thread of the narrative and struggle to maintain a consistent structure across hundreds of pages. In high-stakes domains like scientific publishing, this is compounded by the tendency to hallucinate citations and factual claims, making standard chat interfaces unsuitable for rigorous academic work without external scaffolding.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Advances in context window sizes (e.g., models supporting millions of tokens) and improved Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) techniques are continuously mitigating these limitations. As models improve natively in long-context recall, the "wall" effect may diminish without needing complex external scaffolding.
