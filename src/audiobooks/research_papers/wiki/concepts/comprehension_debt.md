## TLDR

A phenomenon where AI-generated code functions correctly but is too logically convoluted for humans to understand, leading to ecosystem degradation.

## Body

Comprehension debt emerges as a critical vulnerability in software ecosystems managed by autonomous AI agents. It occurs when AI-generated code successfully passes unit tests and functions as intended, but possesses a logical structure so convoluted that human developers cannot understand why or how it works.

This phenomenon represents a severe escalation of traditional technical debt. As AI agents continuously modify, patch, and optimize codebases, the system gradually degrades into an opaque black box. The lack of human readability means that debugging, auditing, or predicting the downstream effects of future changes becomes nearly impossible, setting the stage for unpredictable cascade failures.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

A primary counterargument is the assumption that human comprehension remains a necessary component of future software maintenance. If AI agents become sufficiently advanced at debugging, auditing, and maintaining their own convoluted code, human readability may become an obsolete requirement.

Additionally, proponents of AI-driven development might argue that 'comprehension debt' is a temporary gap. They suggest this gap will soon be bridged by specialized AI interpretability tools and large language models specifically designed to translate complex machine logic back into human-readable summaries.
