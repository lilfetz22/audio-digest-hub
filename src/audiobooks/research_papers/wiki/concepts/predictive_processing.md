## TLDR

A theoretical framework where systems function as active prediction machines that update their internal models only upon encountering prediction errors.

## Body

Predictive processing suggests that the brain is not a passive input-output processor, but an active model generator. Instead of processing raw incoming stimuli in detail, the system generates a prediction of the world and compares it against sensory data.

Only the 'prediction error'—the difference between the prediction and the reality—is propagated through the system to update the internal model. This approach is highly efficient, as it minimizes the need to process redundant information and focuses the system's computational power on novel or unexpected events.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Defining a universal 'prediction error' signal for complex AI systems is challenging, and in many current architectures, calculating these errors can be as computationally expensive as standard backpropagation. There is also the risk of 'hallucinations' if the system relies too heavily on its internal model over actual sensory evidence.
