## TLDR

An evaluation metric used to measure how closely an AI-generated document structure matches the expected academic flow.

## Body

Soft-Heading Recall is a metric designed to evaluate the structural quality of AI-generated academic texts, such as literature reviews. Instead of evaluating the raw text generation or factual accuracy, it specifically focuses on the organizational flow and heading structure of the document.

In the context of the referenced study, the AI system achieved a 98% Soft-Heading Recall rate across 500 multi-domain literature reviews. This high percentage indicates that the system is highly capable of mimicking standard academic structures, ensuring that the generated content follows logical, expected progressions required for scholarly work.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

While a high Soft-Heading Recall indicates excellent structural mimicry, it does not guarantee the factual accuracy, depth of analysis, or novelty of the content beneath those headings. It is strictly a measure of structural alignment, meaning a document could score perfectly on this metric while still containing hallucinated or flawed arguments.
