## TLDR

Higher intelligence in AI models acts as a safety net that prevents catastrophic failures in multi-agent systems, rather than necessarily improving median performance.

## Body

In multi-agent environments, utilizing models with higher average intelligence does not guarantee a better median market outcome. However, it serves a critical risk-management function by drastically reducing the likelihood of catastrophic market failures.

Data scientists and system architects should therefore view advanced model intelligence as a form of "crash protection." It acts as a safety net against the "tail risk" of the system, ensuring stability in extreme or edge cases even if everyday performance remains comparable to that of less capable models.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Relying solely on model intelligence for crash protection may obscure underlying systemic flaws in the multi-agent architecture. Furthermore, highly intelligent models might still fail in unpredictable, correlated ways (e.g., synchronized hallucinations) that could introduce new forms of systemic tail risk.
