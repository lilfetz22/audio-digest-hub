## TLDR

A research methodology that manually codes and maps existing literature across key dimensions to identify systemic logical gaps rather than running new benchmarks.

## Body

Bounded evidence synthesis is a methodological approach utilized to analyze existing literature and standards by mapping them across specific functional dimensions. In this context, the authors manually coded 24 recent papers and standards documents to understand the current landscape of AI system controls.

Instead of running new, potentially fragmented benchmarks, this approach focuses on four critical dimensions: what is measured, what is governed, where control is applied, and what evidence is retained. By evaluating these elements as an interconnected system rather than isolated silos, researchers can effectively identify systemic breakdowns and logical gaps in current practices.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

While bounded evidence synthesis provides a strong theoretical and systemic mapping, it relies heavily on the manual coding of a relatively small sample size (24 papers). This introduces potential selection bias and subjective interpretation by the coders. Furthermore, by avoiding empirical benchmarking, the methodology may miss practical, real-world performance nuances that only emerge during live system testing.
