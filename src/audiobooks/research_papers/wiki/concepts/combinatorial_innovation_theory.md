## TLDR

A theory positing that true novelty arises from the atypical recombination of existing concepts rather than creating something entirely from scratch.

## Body

Combinatorial Innovation Theory suggests that breakthroughs in science and technology are rarely isolated moments of pure invention. Instead, they are typically the result of combining pre-existing ideas, tools, or concepts in novel and atypical ways.

In the context of AI and automated scientific discovery, this theory serves as a foundational heuristic. By mapping out existing knowledge spaces and deliberately forcing intersections between disparate domains, systems can algorithmically simulate human creativity. This shifts the burden of innovation from generating ideas out of "thin air" to effectively searching and merging the combinatorial space of current knowledge.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Critics might argue that while combinatorial innovation explains many incremental or cross-disciplinary advances, it struggles to account for true paradigm shifts or fundamental discoveries that lack clear precedents. Furthermore, the search space for all possible combinations is computationally explosive, making it difficult to identify valuable combinations without generating overwhelming amounts of noise.
