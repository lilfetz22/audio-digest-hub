## TLDR

The effectiveness of AI tools is often overestimated in studies because real-world usage is a voluntary choice influenced by a user's innate skill and motivation rather than the tool itself.

## Body

Endogeneity in the context of human-AI collaboration refers to the correlation between the independent variable (AI access) and the error term (unobserved characteristics of the user). When researchers force AI usage in controlled trials, they ignore the 'self-selection bias' inherent in how individuals decide whether to adopt new technology.

In natural settings, users do not adopt AI randomly. High-performing individuals may utilize AI to refine elite-level strategies, while lower-skilled individuals might avoid it due to intimidation or a lack of understanding. This creates a selection effect where the measured productivity gain is not purely the result of the AI, but a combination of the user's pre-existing aptitude and their choice to seek feedback.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Critics might argue that even if adoption is endogenous, the observed 'net benefit' is the only metric that matters for organizational policy. Furthermore, some argue that the 'self-selection' itself is a productive skill that AI developers should encourage rather than view as a statistical noise factor.
