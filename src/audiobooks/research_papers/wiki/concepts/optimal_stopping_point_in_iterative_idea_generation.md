## TLDR

The practice of halting multi-agent iterative idea generation at a specific point, typically the second iteration, to maximize diversity and prevent path-locking.

## Body

Iterative idea generation using AI agents demonstrates a distinct trade-off between idea quality and conceptual diversity. Research indicates that while the overall structural quality of generated ideas improves with successive iterations, the diversity of these ideas typically peaks at the second iteration.

Beyond this peak, agents tend to experience "path-locking," where they become overly focused on narrow technical trajectories and rely too heavily on inherited entities from previous outputs. To mitigate this, practitioners are advised to establish an optimal stopping point. By stopping the process early or injecting fresh, disparate knowledge, users can prevent the generated ideas from calcifying and avoid over-fitting.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Stopping the iteration process early to preserve diversity inherently sacrifices potential improvements in idea quality, logical consistency, and refinement that could occur in later stages. Additionally, identifying the exact optimal stopping point may vary significantly across different scientific domains and require manual tuning, limiting the autonomy of the generation system.
