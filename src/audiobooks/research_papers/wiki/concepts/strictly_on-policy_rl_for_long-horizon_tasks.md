## TLDR

Using strictly on-policy reinforcement learning—where only data generated by the current policy is used for updates—prevents policy lag and stabilizes training for complex agentic workflows.

## Body

In the context of training AI agents for long-horizon research tasks, the authors found that strictly "on-policy" reinforcement learning is significantly safer and more stable than off-policy methods that reuse older data. In this approach, the model is updated exclusively using rollouts from its current version, and those rollouts are immediately discarded after the update.

This method specifically prevents "policy lag," a phenomenon where training on outdated data causes the model's learning trajectory to destabilize. By ensuring the feedback loop is tightly coupled to the model's current state, the training process remains stable even over extended, complex task horizons.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Strictly on-policy RL is notoriously sample-inefficient because it requires discarding all previous rollouts and constantly generating new data. This can be computationally prohibitive unless paired with an extremely fast and cheap simulation environment, making it difficult to scale in environments where data generation is slow or expensive.
