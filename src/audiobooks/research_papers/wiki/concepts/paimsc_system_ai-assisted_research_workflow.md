## TLDR

A system that automates tedious research tasks while leaving critical scientific reasoning and taste to human researchers.

## Body

The pAI/MSc system represents a pragmatic approach to integrating artificial intelligence into the scientific research process. It operates on the principle of dividing labor between AI and human researchers based on their respective strengths and the current limitations of artificial intelligence.

Specifically, the system automates the "boring" or administrative aspects of research, such as state management, document formatting, and literature sourcing. By handling these repetitive tasks, it acts as an agentic workflow that scales overall productivity without compromising the integrity of the work.

Importantly, the framework preserves the "stubbornly human" elements of research. It requires the human researcher to maintain ownership over scientific taste, deep causal reasoning, and the overarching rigor of the scientific method, acknowledging that AI cannot yet replicate these high-level cognitive functions.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

While effective for scaling productivity, heavily relying on agentic workflows raises questions about the long-term reproducibility of empirical results, especially in sensitive domains like time-series forecasting. Furthermore, automated literature sourcing may inadvertently introduce bias or miss critical context if the underlying models hallucinate or fail to retrieve niche papers.
