## TLDR

AI agents currently excel at automating and optimizing known state-of-the-art solutions but struggle to invent entirely new scientific paradigms.

## Body

The **"Creativity Ceiling"** refers to a current limitation in AI-driven research and prototyping. While AI agents are highly capable of generating competitive algorithms, they tend to converge toward variations of known "gold standards" when such standards exist in the literature or training data.

Effectively, these agents are exceptionally good at automating the *known* state-of-the-art. They can combine, optimize, and apply existing methodologies to new problems with high proficiency. However, they have not yet demonstrated the ability to break through to the "creative breakthrough" phase, where an entirely new paradigm or fundamentally novel mathematical approach to a problem is invented.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Some researchers argue that combining existing concepts in novel ways is the very definition of creativity, making the "ceiling" a subjective boundary. Furthermore, as models scale and reinforcement learning techniques improve (e.g., through open-ended exploration), the boundary of this ceiling may shift, eventually leading to genuine paradigm-shifting discoveries.
