## TLDR

A design paradigm for AI-assisted development that prioritizes user flow and acknowledges the non-linear, unpredictable nature of software engineering.

## Body

Human-in-the-loop (HITL) interfaces in AI-assisted development represent a shift from purely automated code generation to collaborative systems. Rather than viewing the AI as a standalone generator, these interfaces are designed to act as partners that integrate into the developer's messy, iterative workflow.

By respecting non-linear development cycles, these interfaces aim to reduce friction. This involves designing systems that provide assistance without interrupting cognitive flow, ensuring that the AI supports the human developer's decision-making process rather than attempting to replace it entirely.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Critics argue that over-reliance on HITL systems can lead to 'automation bias,' where developers uncritically accept AI suggestions. Additionally, designing interfaces that can handle the chaotic, non-linear nature of real-world software engineering is notoriously difficult to standardize.
