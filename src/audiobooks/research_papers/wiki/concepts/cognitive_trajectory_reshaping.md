## TLDR

The hypothesis that AI tools do not merely automate tasks but fundamentally alter how populations approach cognition and problem-solving.

## Body

The researchers posit that the integration of AI into problem-solving workflows shifts the cognitive burden from the human to the machine. This shift is not neutral; it effectively alters the 'cognitive trajectory' of users by offloading critical thinking processes to automated agents.

There is a concern that this trajectory leads to a reduction in human problem-solving diversity. By prioritizing the 'best' or most efficient answer provided by an AI, societies risk pruning the varied, heuristic-driven approaches that historically underpin human resilience and innovation.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Some scholars argue that offloading cognitive tasks frees human intellect for higher-level creative and strategic synthesis, which could arguably expand human cognitive potential rather than narrow it. Others point out that human problem-solving has always evolved alongside tools, and this shift is simply a modern iteration of cognitive scaffolding.
