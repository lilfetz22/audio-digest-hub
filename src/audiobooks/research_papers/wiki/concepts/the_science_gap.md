## TLDR

The observed disparity in performance and capability when current AI agents are applied to rigorous, autonomous scientific research tasks.

## Body

The "Science Gap" refers to the sobering findings discovered when evaluating a wide array of AI agents on complex scientific tasks. In this specific methodological evaluation, researchers tested 57 different agents spanning 22 distinct architectural classes.

While general-purpose agents may perform well on standard consumer benchmarks, applying them to a rigorous, production-grade scientific research environment reveals significant shortcomings. The term implies that despite the proliferation of various agent architectures and massive language models, current systems struggle to perform autonomously and accurately when faced with the depth and complexity of actual scientific discovery.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

The definition and severity of the "Science Gap" heavily depends on the specific benchmarks chosen and the expectation of autonomy. Some researchers argue that while agents currently fail at fully autonomous discovery, they already excel as "scientific copilots," meaning the gap is more about a lack of workflow integration and autonomy rather than a lack of actual scientific utility.
