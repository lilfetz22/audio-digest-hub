## TLDR

A decision-making strategy where agents work in isolation before sharing ideas, designed to prevent premature consensus and groupthink.

## Body

The Nominal Group Technique (NGT) is a structural intervention used to maintain idea diversity within multi-agent systems. By requiring agents to generate proposals independently before any communication or sharing occurs, the system avoids the influence of early-mover bias or dominant opinions.

This technique acts as a 'speed bump' against premature convergence. By isolating the cognitive process of generation from the social process of consensus, NGT preserves the unique semantic 'modes' that would otherwise be filtered out by early integration or peer pressure.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

NGT may slow down the total throughput of an agentic system, as it requires a multi-phase integration process. It may also lead to redundant efforts where agents spend compute cycles generating overlapping ideas that could have been avoided through iterative, real-time collaboration.
