## TLDR

TPGO represents a paradigm shift from traditional prompt engineering to systems that actively refine their own internal logic and architecture.

## Body

TPGO marks a transition in how artificial intelligence systems and agents are designed and utilized. Instead of relying on static, human-crafted instructions—commonly known as prompt engineering—TPGO focuses on "architectural evolution."

In this paradigm, systems do not merely execute a fixed set of commands. Instead, they are designed to actively analyze, modify, and refine their own internal logic and agent graphs. This allows the system to autonomously improve its problem-solving processes over time, adapting its underlying structure to better handle complex tasks.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

While self-refining systems offer the promise of reduced manual oversight, they can become unpredictable and difficult to debug. Unconstrained architectural evolution might lead to overly complex or inefficient logic graphs that are computationally expensive, potentially introducing compounding errors without human-in-the-loop verification.
