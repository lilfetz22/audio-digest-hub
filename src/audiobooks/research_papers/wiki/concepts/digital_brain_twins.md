## TLDR

The simulation-based strategy of modeling biological nervous systems, starting from simple organisms like the fruit fly, to validate control architectures.

## Body

Digital brain twins represent a methodology of incremental biological modeling. By creating high-fidelity digital replicas of simpler brains, such as those of the fruit fly, researchers gain a sandbox to test and validate control architectures and learning algorithms that are not yet understood in more complex systems.

This roadmap approach suggests that once models prove effective in simpler organisms, they can be scaled in complexity toward mice and beyond. This serves as a vital bridge between neuroscience and AI, allowing engineers to verify whether specific control structures can effectively replicate biological intelligence before attempting to deploy them in synthetic AI models.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Critics argue that current models may oversimplify biological systems, ignoring the chemical and hormonal signaling that play critical roles in brain function. Additionally, the computational cost of simulating a biological system accurately enough to be useful remains prohibitive.
