## TLDR

A memory management mechanism in the Prism system that categorizes and stores agent information based on entropy to optimize context window usage.

## Body

Entropy-gated stratification is a memory management mechanism utilized within Prism, an evolutionary memory substrate designed for multi-agent open-ended discovery. This system evaluates the information generated by agents and categorizes it into different storage tiers based on its informational entropy.

Low-entropy, repeatable procedures are classified as "Skills" and are prioritized to remain always-loaded in the agent's memory. Conversely, higher-entropy or transient data, such as "Notes" and "Attempts," are relegated to secondary storage tiers. This stratification ensures that the agent retains immediate access to critical procedures without overwhelming its active memory.

By systematically filtering the information that enters the active context window, entropy-gated stratification prevents the context from becoming clogged with low-value logs. This is crucial for maintaining the agent's reasoning capabilities and ensuring sustained performance during long-term, open-ended discovery tasks.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Relying on entropy as the primary metric for stratification might inadvertently filter out high-entropy "Notes" or "Attempts" that contain crucial, albeit chaotic, insights necessary for breakthrough discoveries. Furthermore, the computational overhead of constantly calculating entropy and managing dynamic memory tiers could impact the overall speed and efficiency of the multi-agent system.
