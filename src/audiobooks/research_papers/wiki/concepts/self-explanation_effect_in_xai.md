## TLDR

XAI tools should nudge users to generate their own hypotheses about AI behavior rather than just handing them the answer.

## Body

Borrowed from psychology and constructivist learning theories, the self-explanation effect posits that humans learn and retain information much more effectively when they are required to explain a concept themselves.

Applied to the "Producing" stage of Explainable AI, this concept suggests a radical departure from traditional XAI interfaces. Instead of the AI simply delivering the final "answer" or the rationale for its output, the interface should be designed to prompt and challenge the user.

By nudging the user to generate their own hypotheses about why the model behaved in a certain way, the XAI system forces active participation. This methodology transforms the user from a passive recipient of information into an active learner, ultimately leading to a deeper, more robust understanding of the AI's underlying logic and limitations.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Relying on the self-explanation effect assumes the user has sufficient baseline knowledge to generate a valid hypothesis. If a user lacks domain expertise or AI literacy, nudging them to self-explain might reinforce incorrect mental models, lead to frustration, or cause them to guess arbitrarily just to bypass the prompt.
