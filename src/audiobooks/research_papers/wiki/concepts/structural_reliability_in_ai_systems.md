## TLDR

Robust AI systems rely more on well-organized structures and agent interactions than on raw individual model intelligence.

## Body

In the ongoing development of artificial intelligence, there is a pervasive tendency to over-index on raw model capability—focusing primarily on making models larger and inherently "smarter." However, this perspective often neglects the critical role of structural reliability in system design.

This concept argues that instead of constantly pushing for more capable individual models, developers should focus on building better "institutions" or organizational frameworks for existing agents. By carefully structuring how agents interact, process information, and execute tasks, systems can achieve higher robustness and reliability. Ultimately, the most resilient systems are not necessarily the most intelligent at a granular level, but rather the most well-organized.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Creating rigid "institutions" or structural frameworks might bottleneck the emergent, generalized problem-solving capabilities of highly intelligent autonomous models. Furthermore, designing optimal structures for agents is a highly complex, domain-specific task that may not scale or generalize as easily as simply increasing a foundational model's parameter count.
