## TLDR

A phenomenon where autonomous coding agents cause long-term architectural degradation despite producing functional, test-passing code in the short term.

## Body

Agentic Entropy refers to the accumulation of technical debt and architectural decay resulting from the use of autonomous software agents. While these agents are highly effective at navigating file systems and executing immediate tasks to satisfy current test suites, they often lack a holistic understanding of long-term design patterns and system intent.

Because these agents operate primarily on immediate, localized task completion, they frequently introduce duplicated logic and violate established architectural principles. Over time, these small, individually 'correct' modifications compound into a fragmented and brittle codebase that becomes difficult for human engineers to maintain or extend.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Critics argue that this phenomenon is not unique to agents but is a byproduct of poor task definitions or inadequate system prompts provided by human operators. Furthermore, as agents gain better capabilities in context-window management and long-term memory, they may eventually be able to enforce structural consistency better than humans, potentially reducing entropy over time.
