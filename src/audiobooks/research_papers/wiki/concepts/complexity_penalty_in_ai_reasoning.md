## TLDR

AI agents can handle simple information structures but fail at complex, multi-layered logic puzzles in prediction markets.

## Body

In an experiment utilizing 3,500 prediction markets, various Large Language Models (LLMs) were tested on their ability to infer hidden knowledge from price movements. The complexity of the information structures varied from 'Easy' signal games to 'Very Hard' scenarios, such as the classic 'Muddy Children' puzzle, which requires reasoning through multiple layers of counterfactuals.

While AI agents performed perfectly on 'Easy' and 'Medium' structures, they exhibited a severe 'Complexity Penalty' when faced with 'Very Hard' structures. In these complex scenarios, the median market price generated by the agents devolved into random noise. Notably, even state-of-the-art frontier models tested in April 2026 failed to overcome this limitation, hitting a definitive ceiling in their inferential capabilities.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

The failure of LLMs in highly complex scenarios may not strictly indicate an absolute ceiling of intelligence. It could instead reflect limitations in the specific prompt framing, context window constraints, or a lack of specialized reinforcement learning for market environments. Additionally, the 'Very Hard' structures might introduce artificial logic traps that do not perfectly mimic the organic flow of real-world financial markets.
