## TLDR

An interdisciplinary research paradigm that aims to integrate biological neuroscience principles into artificial intelligence architectures to overcome current scaling plateaus.

## Body

NeuroAI posits that the next generation of AI advancement will not come solely from increasing compute or data scale, but from architectural shifts inspired by the brain. Current models, particularly transformers, excel at pattern recognition but lack the foundational cognitive abilities—such as physical world interaction and continuous learning—that biological systems have evolved over millennia.

By leveraging insights from neuroscience, researchers aim to develop models that move beyond passive next-token prediction. This involves designing systems that possess an internal representation of causality, enabling them to navigate dynamic, real-world environments with the efficiency and adaptive intelligence characteristic of biological neural networks.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Critics argue that biological brain emulation is not strictly necessary for achieving Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), suggesting that silicon-based architectures might naturally discover superior, non-biological pathways to intelligence. Additionally, mapping specific neural processes to artificial computational structures is notoriously difficult, risking the implementation of 'biomimicry' that is computationally expensive and functionally unnecessary.
