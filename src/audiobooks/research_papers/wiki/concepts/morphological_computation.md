## TLDR

The principle that a system's physical structure can offload computational tasks from the central controller, effectively utilizing body dynamics to simplify control.

## Body

Morphological computation posits that a robot's physical embodiment—such as the elasticity of a limb or the geometry of a sensor array—performs 'computation' by responding to environmental physics without the need for active algorithmic control. This concept emphasizes the co-design of body and controller rather than treating the hardware as a mere passive vessel for software.

By leveraging the natural physical dynamics of the system, architects can reduce the complexity of the software controller. This results in more energy-efficient machines that naturally handle tasks like stability or object manipulation, mirroring the way biological organisms rely on skeletal and muscular morphology to simplify locomotion.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Designing robots that rely on specific physical dynamics can limit their versatility, as the software becomes tightly coupled to the hardware constraints. A system optimized for one set of physical parameters may struggle to adapt if the morphology needs to change to perform a different task.
