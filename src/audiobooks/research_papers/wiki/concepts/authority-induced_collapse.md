## TLDR

A phenomenon where hierarchical multi-agent systems suffer from diversity loss as subordinate agents align their outputs to match the leader to avoid conflict.

## Body

Authority-Induced Collapse occurs in hierarchical agent structures where a central 'leader' dictates or influences the output of 'junior' agents. Because the subordinates aim to minimize disagreement or maximize compliance, their vector representations gravitate toward the leader's position, effectively erasing the potential for divergent thinking.

This collapse manifests as a loss of semantic dispersion. Even if the junior agents are individually capable, the structural pressure to conform results in a system that produces uniform, 'sycophantic' research proposals rather than challenging or novel perspectives.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Some argue that hierarchical structures are necessary for goal-directed tasks, and that preventing collapse requires better task delegation or incentive alignment rather than the abandonment of hierarchy. There is also the counter-perspective that 'leader' agents can act as stabilizers to prevent the production of high-entropy, low-utility garbage.
