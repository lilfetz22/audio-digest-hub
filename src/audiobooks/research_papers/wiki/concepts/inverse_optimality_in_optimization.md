## TLDR

A method for generating algorithms by performing control jumps that reduce a search energy rather than solving complex partial differential equations.

## Body

Inverse optimality reverses the traditional paradigm of optimization, where one typically derives an algorithm from the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equations. Instead, it allows for the synthesis of algorithms by identifying discrete control jumps that satisfy stability constraints.

By ensuring that every step is a 'descent' step regarding the search energy, the system bypasses the need to solve computationally expensive PDEs. This allows for the systematic construction of algorithms that are intrinsically stable and convergent by design.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

While this approach avoids explicit PDE solvers, it relies heavily on the availability of a suitable energy function, which may not always exist or be easily differentiable. Additionally, the 'optimal' path generated by these discrete jumps may not align with the global optimum if the energy function possesses unwanted local minima.
