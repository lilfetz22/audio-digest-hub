## TLDR

A three-layer architectural framework designed to ground autonomous agents in specific operational, domain-specific, and interaction-based contexts.

## Body

Foundation AgenticOS (FAOS) operates as a meta-framework that moves beyond generic LLM prompting by organizing agent behavior through a tripartite ontology. By categorizing information into role-based perspectives, domain-specific knowledge, and workflow-based interaction protocols, the system creates a structured constraint environment. This allows the agent to maintain consistency across complex professional tasks that require adherence to external organizational logic.

The framework is designed to bridge the gap between abstract model reasoning and practical execution. By enforcing these ontological boundaries, FAOS reduces hallucinations and misaligned outputs, ensuring the agent prioritizes the specific metrics and decision-making logic relevant to its assigned persona while navigating rigid industry requirements.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

The primary limitation of FAOS is the overhead required to construct and maintain the ontologies for every specific domain. As industry regulations or internal protocols evolve, the ontologies may become outdated, requiring costly manual updates or expert-in-the-loop maintenance.
