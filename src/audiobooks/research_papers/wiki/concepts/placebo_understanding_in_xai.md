## TLDR

Overly simplified AI explanations can create a false sense of comprehension that leads to dangerous automation bias.

## Body

In the "Receiving" stage of human-AI interaction, the cognitive processing of an explanation is critical. When XAI systems provide overly simplistic explanations aimed purely at user satisfaction, they risk inducing a phenomenon known as "placebo understanding."

Placebo understanding occurs when a user feels confident that they understand how an AI system works, even though their comprehension is superficial or fundamentally flawed. This false confidence is dangerous because it directly contributes to automation bias, where users over-rely on the AI's judgments without applying proper critical oversight.

To combat this, XAI design must pivot from optimizing for user satisfaction to optimizing for "cognitive engagement." Explanations should appropriately reflect the complexity of the model to ensure the user is actively processing the information rather than passively accepting an illusion of transparency.

## Counterarguments / Data Gaps

Designing for "cognitive engagement" by increasing explanation complexity risks alienating non-expert users, who may abandon the system entirely if they feel overwhelmed. Striking the perfect balance between preventing placebo understanding and maintaining system usability remains a difficult, highly context-dependent challenge.
